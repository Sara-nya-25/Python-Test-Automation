Classic Creamy Scrambled Eggs

There is nothing quite like a perfectly cooked scrambled egg for breakfast. The secret lies in low heat and patience—resulting in a silky, luxurious start to your day.

Ingredients

  • 3 large organic eggs
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream (or whole milk)
  • A pinch of sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions

1. Preparation

Start by cracking the eggs into a small bowl. Add the cream and a tiny pinch of salt. Whisk gently with a fork until the yolks and whites are just combined, but avoid whisking too much—we don't want too many air bubbles.

2. The Cooking Process

Melt the butter in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Once the butter stops foaming, pour in the egg mixture. Let it sit for a few seconds until the bottom begins to set.

Using a silicone spatula, gently push the eggs from the edges toward the center. Continue to stir slowly and steadily. Keeping the heat low is vital; if the pan is too hot, the eggs will become dry and rubbery.

3. Finishing and Serving

Remove the pan from the heat while the eggs still look slightly wet and glossy. The residual heat will finish the cooking. Top with freshly ground pepper and finely chopped chives. Serve immediately, ideally on a piece of buttered sourdough toast.
